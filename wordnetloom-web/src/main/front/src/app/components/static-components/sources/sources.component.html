The Online Dictionary of Polish Borrowings in Yiddish

The main goal of our investigation was not to create an exhaustive dictionary but rather to establish the grade of rootedness of Polish loanwords in Yiddish. Therefore, the lexicographical study was based on a representative sample of relatively comprehensive and varied sources. That is why the list contains not only well established borrowings but also vocabulary hitherto not recorded in dictionaries and nonce borrowings, i.e. words occurring occasionally due to code switching.

The sources of our corpus can be divided into four main categories:

  1. Printed, predominantly bilingual, Yiddish dictionaries. Following the findings of previous lexicographical undertakings was crucial in order to gather the basic Yiddish vocabulary and also to recognise their shortcomings.
  2. Vocabulary lists published in academic works. Utilising vocabulary gathered for the purpose of a particular case study provides an insight into specific domains or language varieties, for instance the domain of botanics or the tailors’ jargon.
  3. Archival sources. In this study they are represented by a selection of Yiddish writing from the Emanuel Ringelblum Warsaw Ghetto Archive. Together with the previous category, these can give an insight into the vernacular language - an important addition to lexicography which is always a product of a specific language planning project.
  4. Online sources. This category includes the most varied selection of works, among them online dictionaries and databases of research projects.
